# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to making participation in this project respectful, constructive, and welcoming for contributors and users.

## Expected Behavior

- Use clear and respectful language.
- Focus feedback on the work, evidence, and user outcomes.
- Assume good intent while staying precise about technical concerns.
- Respect maintainers' time by providing reproducible details, relevant context, and concise requests.
- Help keep issues, pull requests, and examples useful for the broader community.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, insults, or personal attacks.
- Discriminatory language or conduct.
- Spam, repeated low-effort comments, or off-topic promotion.
- Publishing private information, credentials, or personal data without permission.
- Deliberately misleading claims about project behavior, affiliation, or results.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments, issues, pull requests, and other contributions that do not meet these standards. Maintainers may also limit participation when behavior is disruptive or unsafe for the project community.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns through GitHub's reporting tools or by contacting a repository maintainer. Reports will be reviewed with discretion and handled in proportion to the situation.
